Inferring Feelings and Traits

Good readers are like detectives. They look closely at what the characters say, do, and how they look. They try to know how the characters feel and what kind of people they are.

The following are clues to better understand the characters:

  1. Words: words does the author use to describe the character’s face or body? Are the words smiling, frowning, or shaking used? These words can give clues about how the character feels.

  2. Actions: What are the characters doing? Are they helping someone? Are they shouting or crying? Their actions can show us what kind of person they are and how they feel.

  3. Thoughts: Does the author tell what the character is thinking? This can be a direct clue to their feelings and what they might do next.

By paying attention to the details of what you read, you can understand the characters’ feelings and behavior.
