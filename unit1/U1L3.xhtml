Birthday Surprises
Amy R. Pomoy

“Today is a special day! It is finally my birthday! I am now six years old.” Elise thought to herself as soon as she woke up.

She fixed her bed and went out for breakfast.

Today is also a holiday. This means that Elise does not have to go to school. She gets to spend time with her family doing the things she wants. It is really a special day!

Why is it a special day? What is Elise planning on doing that day?

Everything was quiet when she got to the dining room. Mommy and Daddy, and her little brother, Nico, were not around. Instead, Elise found a piece of paper on the table. On it was a drawing and a short note.

Elise studied the drawing on the paper. “I think I need to go out the door to our yard!”

She quickly went to their back door and walked hurriedly to their yard.

“Surprise! Happy birthday!” Mommy, Daddy, and Nico shouted as soon as they saw Elise. They were all in their pajamas, wearing party hats and holding balloons. There was a big table with all of Elise’s favorite breakfast food—pancakes, scrambled eggs, toast, and fruits. There was also a big pitcher of juice and a carton of milk. In the middle was a pink and purple birthday cake with lots of sugar flowers. “Happy birthday to you, happy birthday to you,” they began singing. Elise had a big smile on her face. After blowing out all six candles, she hugged her Mommy, Daddy, and Nico.

“It’s a holiday, so we decided that we will spend your birthday at home.” Daddy explained.

“Ate, were you surprised? I fixed the table for you!” Nico said.

“Of course, a birthday will not be complete without a gift,” Mommy said as she brought out a box with a pink ribbon from behind her. “Happy birthday, Sweetheart!”

Elise was so happy to receive her present. She hugged her mommy tightly and whispered, “I love you! This is the best birthday ever!”

GRAMMAR

Expressing Cause and Effect

Sometimes, one event can make another event happen. This relationship between events is called cause and effect. The cause is what makes something happen and the effect is the result of it.

Study the following pictures. Why is the family planning a surprise party? What is the result of the surprise party?

Cause : The family planned a surprise for Elise’s birthday.
Effect : Elise felt surprised and happy.

You can use the following words to talk about why things happen and what happens next:

because—to express the cause or reason why something happened

so—to express effect or result

Read the following sentences with your teacher.

Because it was Elise’s birthday, her family planned a surprise party.

The family prepared a surprise birthday party so Elise would be happy.
