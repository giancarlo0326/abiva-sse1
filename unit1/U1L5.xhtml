Asking Questions to Clarify Information

Listen as your teacher reads the following sentences. Tell what is the same about them.

Who is your favorite singer?
What is your talent?
Where do you like to dance?
When do you show your talent?
Why do you enjoy painting?

All the sentences above end with a question mark (?).
Sentences that end with a question mark are questions or asking
sentences. They are used to ask for information. Most questions
begin with who, what, where, when, and why.

Who is used when the answer to the question is a person.
What is used when the answer to the question is a thing.
Where is used when the answer to the question is a place.
When is used when the answer to the question is a day, date, or time.
Why is used when the answer to the question is a reason.

Identifying Problem and Solution

Finding Out Your Talent
Amy R. Pomoy

Paolo is trying to figure out what he is good at but he does not know where to start.

He tried painting, but it did not feel right. Next, he played drums, but he could not find the right beat.

Then, he joined the dance club. He liked the way he could make his body move with the music so easily. He felt happy every time he danced. His friends also cheer when he performs.

Now, Paolo practices every day to become a better dancer.

Remember, it is okay to not know what you are good at right away. Keep trying new things. You might discover your special talent!

In the story, Paolo had a hard time figuring out where he is good at and what he likes to do. This is called a problem in the story. The problem in a story is what the character needs to fix or find out. It could also show what goes wrong in the story.

Paolo tried different things to solve his problem. This is called the solution. It is how the character solves the problem or makes things right again.
