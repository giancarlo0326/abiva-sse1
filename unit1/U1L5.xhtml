SPEAKING

Asking Questions to Clarify Information

Listen as your teacher reads the following sentences. Tell what is the same about them.

Who is your favorite singer?
What is your talent?
Where do you like to dance?
When do you show your talent?
Why do you enjoy painting?

All the sentences above end with a question mark (?).
Sentences that end with a question mark are questions or asking
sentences. They are used to ask for information. Most questions
begin with who, what, where, when, and why.

Who is used when the answer to the question is a person.
What is used when the answer to the question is a thing.
Where is used when the answer to the question is a place.
When is used when the answer to the question is a day, date, or time.
Why is used when the answer to the question is a reason.
