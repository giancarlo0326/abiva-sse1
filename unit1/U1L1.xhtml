Sally Has Many Names
Anonymous

Sally woke up with a big stretch. Today is going to be a fun day at school! Sally’s mom called out, “Good morning, Darling! Are you ready for breakfast?” Sally smiled. Sally’s mom always calls her Darling.

At the doorstep, Sally’s little sister, Claire, came running. “Hi, Ate!” Claire greeted Sally. “Hi Claire, are you ready for school?” Sally giggled.

Who calls Sally, Darling?
Who else will give Sally a different name?

On the bus, Sally sat next to her best friend, Sammy. “Hey, Best Friend!” Sammy called out. Sally and Sammy talked excitedly about their favorite books.

At school, Ms. Rodriguez welcomed everyone. “Good morning, pupils! Today, we are learning about colors!”

What does her teacher call her and her classmates?
What will her other family members call her?

After school, Sally’s grandparents were waiting for her. “Good afternoon, Lolo and Lola.” Sally pressed her forehead on the back of her grandparents’ hands. Sally’s lola hugged her tightly. “Hello, Apo,” her lola said. Sally’s lolo winked. “Ready to go home, Hija?” Sally grinned and said, “Yes, thank you.”

Sally loves spending time with her family and friends.

Noting Details in a Story

Reading a story is like going on an adventure. You meet interesting people or animals. You get to see the different places that they visit. You also get to learn what happens to them.

A story is made up of the following important details:

Characters. These are the actors in the story. They can be either people or animals.

Setting. This is where the story takes place. It can be as simple as a house or as wild as a jungle.

Events. These are the things that happen in the beginning, middle, and end of the story. At the beginning of a story, you meet the characters and see where they are. In the middle part of the story, some problems happen and the characters try to solve them. The end tells how the story finishes.
