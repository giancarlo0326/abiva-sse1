SPEAKING

Greeting and Responding Appropriately

When you see your friends and teachers, it is important to use friendly greetings! You can say, “Hello” or “Good morning” with a smile. When someone greets you, you should always respond nicely. You might say “Hi!” or “Hello to you too!”

Remember to look at the person and use a cheerful voice. Being polite when you greet others helps you make friends and shows that you care about them.

A.
Read this dialogue with your teacher.

A Morning at the Park
Thelma Grace Abiva-Leung

It was a bright, sunny morning. Mia and her mom went to the park in their subdivision.

Mom: Good morning, Mr. Ramos!

Mr. Ramos: Good morning to you, too! How are you both today?

Mom: We are well—thank you! And how are you, Mr. Ramos?

Mr. Ramos: I am fine. Thank you.

They walked and saw Mia’s friend Jake playing with blocks on the table.

Mia: Hi, Jake!

Jake: Hello, Mia! Nice to see you!

Mia: What are you doing?

Jake: I am building a tower with these blocks.

They noticed Jake dropped one block on the floor.

Mia: Oh, Jake. Your red block fell on the floor. Do you need help getting it?

Jake: Yes, please! I cannot reach it.

Mia: (reaches carefully) Here it is!

Jake: Thank you so much, Mia!

Mom: That was kind of you to help, Mia.

Mia: You are welcome!

Then they saw Sarah sitting quietly nearby, looking at her ball in the pond.

Mia: Hello, Sarah!

Sarah: Hi, Mia.

Mia: Do you want to play with us?

Sarah: I am a bit sad—I dropped my ball in the pond.

Jake: Oh no! Do you want us to fetch it?

Mia: Yes! I will ask Mom for help.

Mia: Mom, could you please help us get Sarah’s ball from the pond?

Mom: Of course. I will use a stick to reach it.

Mom reaches the ball and hands it back to Sarah.

Sarah: Thank you so much, Tita!

Mom: You are welcome!

Sarah: Let us all play together now!

Jake: Yes—let us go to the swings!

Can you share some of the blessings you received this year? How did you feel when you received them? Have you ever thought of sharing your blessings with others? In the story, you will find out who shared his blessings with others.

Blessings in a Box
Thelma Grace Abiva-Leung

Dear Lolo Louie,

Hi, Lolo! How are you?

I just wanted to write to you about what I did yesterday. I went to our parish with my friends and shared my blessings with street children. It all started when my teacher asked us in class, “Who do you want to serve and help this year?” Then my teacher showed us videos about the people who need help, like the homeless, street children, and orphans.

This question really made me think. It also reminded me of seeing you and Lola going to the Home for the Aged every year to donate grocery items and clothes. I realized I wanted to be like you and Lola, and do something for people who are in need. I asked my friends, who lived in my barangay, to join me, and we decided to help the street children. Papa and Mama also helped me organize this project.

What did John decide to do? How will they do it?

My friends and I started to plan what we should do, and we decided to name our outreach project “Blessings in a Box.”

The plan was to fill up shoeboxes with toys, clothes, books, and grocery goods to give to the children who live on the streets in our community.

First, we had a garage sale to raise class funds.

Next, we asked for donations from schoolmates, family, and friends for new or preloved toys, books, and clothes.

Then, with the money earned, we went to the grocery store and bought goods like milk, cookies, and canned goods.

After that, we sorted everything and filled up 50 shoeboxes with toys, clothes, books, and food.

What were their plans? What happened with their plans?

Finally, we asked the parish to help us invite 50 street children from our barangay.

Yesterday, my friends and I were so excited. We went to the parish early to set up everything.

Fifty (50) street children came, and we played games and gave them food.

Before we ended, we gave each child a shoebox. They were so happy!

My friends and I were so tired but we felt happy. Seeing the happy faces of the children made all our efforts worthwhile.

It was a lot of hard work, but we know we did something that helped other people.

I think I will do this again next year.

Love,
John

Sequencing Stories

Have you ever wondered how to tell someone about things that happened in your day? Time words like first, next, then, and last help us tell stories in order. These special words are like a map that shows what happened at the beginning, middle, and end of any event.

When you use time words, your friends and family can better understand when things happened.

Think about getting ready for school in the morning.

First, you wake up and get out of bed. Next, you brush your teeth and wash your face. Then, you put on your clothes for the day. Last, you eat breakfast and grab your backpack.

Using these words helps everyone know exactly what you did and when you did it. Time words make our stories clear and easy to follow!
