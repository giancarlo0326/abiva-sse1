SPEAKING

Expressing Ideas in Symbols

Listen as your teacher reads the dialogue.

Teacher: Good morning, class! Today’s lesson is about community symbols. Who would like to share a symbol they have seen in the community?

Ben: Me, Teacher! I went on a “symbol adventure” with my dad yesterday!

Teacher: A symbol adventure? That sounds exciting! Tell us about it, Ben.

Ben: First, we walked past a tall building. I saw a big blue sign with a giant letter H on it.

Teacher: What symbol is that?

Ben: That is the hospital symbol! I saw it in the morning when we visited my grandma. It means doctors and nurses are there to help sick or hurt people.

Teacher: Very good! What did you see next?

Ben: We kept walking, and I saw a sign with a picture of a bus. It was the bus stop symbol.

Teacher: Where did you see that?

Ben: I saw it near the grocery store. It tells people, “You can wait for the bus here!”

Teacher: Great job! Did you discover one more symbol?

Ben: Yes, Teacher! I saw the children crossing symbol. It had pictures of two children walking.

Teacher: And what does that symbol tell us?

Ben: It means, “Be careful! Children cross here.” I saw it near our school. Dad said drivers slow down when they see that symbol to keep kids safe.

Teacher: Wonderful, Ben! You really had an adventure.

Ben: Yes, Teacher! Now, when I see symbols, it feels like they are talking to me!

Blanca's Walk
Amy R. Pomoy

Blanca went out for a walk one afternoon.

She crossed the street and turned at the corner.

Where do you think is Blanca going? Why is the dog following her?

She passed the building with the sweet-smelling bread.

Then she came to the spot where ringing bells filled the air.

The red then turned green where Blanca sat and waited.

What places did Blanca pass? What was she waiting for? Will the dog pass her by?

Then with a whistle and a wave, feet started moving, stepping on white lines as they went.

The pair of feet she loved stopped right where she was, then with gentle hands scooped and carried her up.
