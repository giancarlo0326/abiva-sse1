Our Invisible Heroes
Thelma Grace Abiva-Leung

Yanni always noticed that Daddy and Mommy would always greet and chat with the security guards whenever they enter a building. They would make sure to find out the name of our waiter whenever they were in a restaurant. They would give drinks and snacks to the garbage collectors and the delivery men.

Yanni asked them why they did all these.

Daddy said, “We should be thankful for them. They are the invisible heroes in our community.”

Yanni was surprised when Daddy said that they were invisible and said, “Why are they invisible? I can see and talk with them.”

Daddy laughed. He explained, “Invisible heroes are people who make a big contribution to the community but are not often recognized or acknowledged by the people.”

Yanni immediately remembered and said, “Oh! Like the security guards, waiters, and garbage collectors.”

What did Daddy say about invisible heroes? What did Yanni remember?

Daddy answered, “Yes, but there are more invisible heroes in our community.”

Yanni was surprised! She exclaimed, “There are?” Daddy continued to give examples.

“There are street sweepers that keep our roads clean. Nurses and doctors who help us get better when we are sick. Firemen who put out fires. Policemen who catch criminals and keep us safe. Volunteers in the home for the elderly or in the orphanages. Caregivers who take care of the elderly and sick people. Environmental workers who help save our planet. There are so many out there!”

Yanni realized that she had met so many invisible heroes and never really showed interest in them.

Yanni said, “Oh! That is why you are so nice and friendly to them. Wow! I did not know there were so many invisible heroes in our community.”

Since then, Yanni became more aware of these invisible heroes and made it a point to show her appreciation.

What did Yanni realize? What will Yanni do whenever she sees an invisible hero?

Now, when Yanni sees them, she greets and talks with them. Yanni also says a short thanksgiving prayer and a blessing for them.

Yanni said, “A little bit of kindness and appreciation doesn’t take much effort, but it has a big impact on other people.”

Inferring the Character’s Feelings and Traits

Characters in a story can feel happy, sad, angry, scared, or excited. Their feelings can be understood by looking at their faces, actions, and words. For example, if a character is smiling and jumping, he or she might be happy. If a character is crying, he or she might be sad.

Characters also have traits, these are words that describe the kind of person they are in the story. You can guess traits by observing how they act. For example, a character who helps others may be kind, and one who keeps trying even when things are hard may be brave or hardworking.

When reading, use the clues from pictures, actions, and words to know how a character feels and what kind of person they are.

Read the situation. Then, draw an emoji to show the correct feeling of the character in the circle.

Identifying Cause and Effect

The cause is the reason why something happens. The effect is what happens because of that reason. Words like because, so, since, or therefore help you identify the effect in a sentence.

Read the story. Then, study the following sentences with underlined cause and effect.

Mr. Rafi Got Wet
Thelma Grace Abiva-Leung

One sunny morning, Mr. Rafi, the hardworking construction worker, started fixing the new playground. He wore his bright yellow helmet and carried his heavy tools.

The sky was clear, and the air felt cool. “It is a good day to build!” said Mr. Rafi with a big smile.

After a while, he saw some dark clouds above. “It might rain,” he thought. But Mr. Rafi wanted to finish the playground for the children, so he kept working.

Soon, raindrops began to fall. Mr. Rafi did not stop. Because he kept working in the rain, he got wet!

Soon, the rain stopped, and the children started going to the playground to watch Mr. Rafi finish it.

When he finally finished, the children clapped and said, “Thank you, Mr. Rafi, for building our playground!”

Mr. Rafi smiled and said, “Rain or shine, I will always help our community!”

GRAMMAR

Listening to Short Texts

You listen to short texts for different reasons. Sometimes, you listen to learn new information. Sometimes, you listen to enjoy a story or poem. Other times, you listen because the speaker wants you to do or believe something. Listening carefully helps you understand the purpose of the text.

Your teacher will read a short story about the invisible heroes in our school. Pay attention to the people in the story and what they do.

Our School Heroes
Thelma Grace Abiva-Leung

It was a bright morning, and Mia and Jake were excited. They wore their clean uniforms and walked to school with happy smiles.

When they arrived, the friendly security guard opened the big gate and said, “Good morning!” The children waved and smiled back.

Inside the school, the kind janitor was cleaning the shiny floor. “Thank you for keeping our school clean,” said Mia. The janitor smiled and said, “You’re welcome!”

At lunchtime, they saw the helpful canteen lady serving warm food. The busy nurse checked on a student who felt a little sick.

After class, the strong street sweeper was cleaning the dusty road outside the school. Jake said, “They all work so hard every day.” Mia nodded and said, “They are our invisible heroes!”
