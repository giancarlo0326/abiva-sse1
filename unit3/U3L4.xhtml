LISTENING

Changing Sounds to Make New Words

A new word can be formed when a letter is changed. Letters changed can be found at the beginning, middle, or end of the word.

A.
Listen as your teacher reads the poem.

Our Happy Community
Thelma Grace Abiva-Leung

We live on a street,
Where neighbors we meet,
We smile and say, “Hi!”
As they walk right by.

At the market, we shop,
We buy food and hop,
The baker can bake,
A cake we can make.

The park is so cool,
With a sparkling blue pool,
We run, we have fun, we play,
Together each day.

There are heroes we see,
Who help you and me,
You can be one too,
In all that you do!

Our community’s sweet,
It’s where friends all meet,
Let’s help, let’s care,
And share everywhere!

SPEAKING

Using Action Words

Action words are words that tell what a person, animal, or thing does. They show an action—like run, play, jump, help, or sing.

A.
Read the story “Helping Hands in Our Community.” Underline the words that tell an action.

Helping Hands in Our Community
Thelma Grace Abiva-Leung

It was a bright and sunny day. Everyone in the neighborhood wanted to help and make their community a better place!

Ana swept the street with her broom. “I will make our street clean!” she said with a big smile.

In the park, Ben planted flowers. “These flowers will make the park so pretty!” he said as he dug little holes in the ground.

Mr. Cruz painted the walls of the barangay center. “The bright colors will make everyone happy!” he said as he brushed red and yellow paint.

At the market, Lisa carried bags of food to give to people in need. “This food will help everyone feel strong!” she said while sharing her fruits and vegetables.

Even the kids helped! They picked up trash, watered plants, and helped their parents. “Our community is a great place because we work together!” they cheered.

Everyone was happy because they knew that when you help your community—whether in big or small ways—you make a big difference.

Even small actions can have a big impact, and each helper is a hero in their own way.

Together, they made their community a better and happier place to live!
