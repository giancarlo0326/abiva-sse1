Each letter has a corresponding sound. Words are made up of letters. To be able to read words, you need to sound out each letter and blend them.

/b/ - /u/ - /s/

Identifying Cause and Effect

Have you ever wondered why things happen? Sometimes, one thing happens, and then another thing happens because of it. For example, if you leave your trash on the floor, someone can slip on it.

The reason something happens is called the cause, and what happens because of it is called the effect.

Read the examples:

Cause: Jake helped the elderly cross the street.
Effect: The elderly passed the street safely.
Cause: My family prepared a donation drive for the rescued families.
Effect: The rescued families had enough clothes and food for the next days.

Identifying the Problem and the Solution

In stories, characters often face problems that they need to solve. A problem is something that makes things difficult for a character. The solution is how the character fixes the problem. For example, if a character is hungry, they might make a sandwich as a solution. When you read, look for what is bothering the character and how they make things better. This helps you understand what is happening in the story!
