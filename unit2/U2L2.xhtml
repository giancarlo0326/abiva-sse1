Changing Sounds in Words to Make New Words

Listen carefully as your teacher reads the narrative poem.

The Busy Morning at School
Anonymous

It is a bright, bright morning at Happy Hill School,
The guard opens the gate—he is kind and cool.
The teacher smiles and says, “Good morning, too!”
“Come in, my dear students, there is much to do!”
The janitor sweeps with a shiny mop,
He cleans the floor—he never stops!
The nurse checks bags and helps with care,
Keeping us healthy everywhere.
Everyone is busy, doing their part,
Helping each day with a happy heart!

Every word is made up of sounds. We can hear the first sound or beginning sound, and also the last sound or ending sound in a word.

Example:
The word mop has the sounds /m/, /o/, /p/.
The beginning sound is /m/.
The ending sound is /p/.

New words can be made by changing one sound in a word.

Example:
If you change the /m/ in mop to /t/, it becomes top.

Can you think of another word by changing the beginning or ending sound in the word mop? Share it with your seatmate.

The Helpers in My School
Amy R. Pomoy

Rina happily entered the school gate. She greeted Kuya Rommel with a wave. He is the school guard. He helps the students cross the street safely. He also knows all of them by name! How cool is that?

Rina passed by the canteen on the way to her classroom. She can already smell the food. “I think we will have pancakes for snacks today,” she guessed. She waved to Ate Rose, the canteen caretaker.

Who did Rina meet first? What did he do? What food did Rina smell? Who prepared them? Who will Rina meet next?

Then the bell rang. Rina went to her place in the line. She sang “Lupang Hinirang,” our national anthem, with the rest of the students. Mr. Reyes, the school principal, sang along with them. He even recited the “Panunumpa sa Watawat” and “Panatang Makabayan.” He also made sure that all the students and teachers were doing it properly.

Mrs. Constantino, Rina’s homeroom teacher, led them all to the classroom after. She readily helps those who need assistance in preparing their things for the lesson. She is also the music teacher, so she always begins with a song. The class all happily sang along. It was a great way to start the morning.

During recess, Rina remembered that she needed to borrow a book from the library. Ms. Alfonso, the school librarian, helped her find the book that she needed. It was a good thing that she was there because Rina still had time to eat the pancakes that Ate Rose made. They were yummy!

Who sang the “Lupang Hinirang” with them? Who is Mrs. Constantino? What did Rina remember to do during recess? Who helped her? Who else will Rina meet in school?

Josh, Rina’s classmate, complained of a stomachache while they were answering their workbooks. Rina volunteered to bring him to the clinic. Nurse Jenny was there to help make Josh comfortable. Nurse Jenny always has the sweetest smile. She can make students feel better, like magic!

On the way back to the classroom, Rina noticed Kuya Jonjon mopping the hallway. He warned her to be careful because the floor was slippery. It was a good thing that Kuya Jonjon was there to help keep our school clean.

Finally, it was time to go home. The class said their prayers and goodbyes. “Goodbye, Mrs. Constantino! Goodbye, classmates! Goodbye, school! See you tomorrow!”
