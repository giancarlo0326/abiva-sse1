Words That Show Respect and Friendship at School

Every morning, pupils greet their classmates and teachers. The words they use show respect and friendship.

Listen as your teacher reads the dialogue aloud. See how they make everyone feel happy and welcome.

A Kind Morning in Class
Anonymous

Teacher Mia: Good morning, class!
Students: Good morning, Teacher Mia!
Teacher Mia: How are you today?
Anna: I am happy, Teacher!
Liam: Me too! Thank you for helping me with my homework yesterday.
Teacher Mia: You are welcome, Liam! That is very polite of you to say.
Ben: Excuse me, Teacher Mia. May I borrow a pencil?
Teacher Mia: Yes, you may, Ben. Please return it after class.
Ben: Thank you, Teacher!
Anna: Ben, here is my extra pencil. You can use it, too!
Ben: Thanks, Anna! You are a good friend.
Liam: Let us share our crayons later when we color!
Anna: Sure! Let us help each other.
Teacher Mia: I am proud of you, class. You are using polite and kind words that show respect and friendship.
Students: Thank you, Teacher Mia!

What do you bring when you go to school? Who brings you to school? How do you get there? Do you like what you do in school? Is it better than being at home?

My Second Home
Amy R. Pomoy

I pack my bag—notebooks, books, and pencils—check!
I make sure I have my sandwich, fruit, and drink.
I look in the mirror before I leave,
I pat my collar and my sleeve.

What was in the bag? Why did he look at himself in the mirror? Where do you think the boy is going?

With a bounce and a bye, I go
With my bag and all my things in tow.
Sometimes I walk, sometimes I ride,
But I am always with a smile so wide.

I am leaving my house and going to school
I believe that is so cool!
I see Nanay, waving and saying, “Bye, Jerome!”
As I make my way to my second home.

“My Second Home” is an example of a narrative poem. A narrative poem tells a story. It has a beginning, a middle, and an end, just like a storybook. It can have rhyming words and is fun to read aloud. In a narrative poem, some characters can be people or animals, and something happens to them, like going on an adventure! Sometimes, a narrative poem teaches a lesson or it simply shares a happy feeling.
