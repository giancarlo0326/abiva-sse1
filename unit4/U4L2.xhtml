Getting Ready

Do you have a brother or sister? Do you have features that are the same? What about features that are different?

A Little Help Goes a Long Way
Thelma Grace Abiva-Leung

“These are my twins,” Mama Marisa said, smiling.

Francis and Carlos are twins. They look exactly the same, and it is hard to tell them apart. They both have curly hair, big, round eyes, and a big smile. But once you get to know them, you’ll see they are completely different!

Francis loves the outdoors, while Carlos likes staying inside.

Francis: Let’s go outside and play soccer!

Carlos: I’d rather stay inside and read a book.

Even their favorite foods are different!

Francis: “I want to eat burgers and fries.”

Carlos: “I like eating fish and vegetables.”

Francis enjoys wearing sporty clothes, while Carlos prefers flashy outfits.

“I really appreciate their differences because they are two unique people,” Mama Marisa said.

One day, after school, Francis and Carlos ran home, excited to share what they had learned.

What were the differences between Francis and Carlos? What did Francis and Carlos learn in school that made them work together?

Francis: Mama! Our teacher taught us that we must take care of the Earth because it’s our only home!

Carlos: Yes, Mama! And guess what? We can help by sorting our trash properly. Paper, glass, tin cans, and food waste should be separated.

Francis: And we should follow the 4 R’s!

Mama Marisa: The 4 R’s? What do they mean?

Francis and Carlos answered together:

Refuse–Say no to plastic straws and other wasteful items.

Reuse–Use water bottles and glass containers again.

Reduce–Save resources, like using both sides of a paper.

Recycle–Turn plastic bottles into pencil holders or other useful things.

Francis: Mama, we should do this at home! I don’t want Earth to disappear.

Carlos: I agree! We’ll help, and we can even tell our neighbors. A little help goes a long way, especially when we work together to make a difference.

Mama Marisa was so proud of her twins. They were working together to help save the Earth!

At school, Francis and Carlos started a club called “Eco Warriors.” Many of their classmates joined!

First, they made posters about trash segregation and the 4 R’s.

Next, they reminded their schoolmates to throw trash properly.

Lastly, they shared ways to take care of the Earth!

Francis and Carlos were different in many ways, but they worked together for something important.

Mama Marisa was very proud of her twins!

Let’s all be Eco Warriors and help save the Earth!

VOCABULARY

Recognizing How Language Reflects Cultural Practices and Norms

Lila’s Language Adventure
Thelma Grace Abiva-Leung

Lila loved listening to the different words people used every day.

At home, Lila spoke Bisaya with her Mama and Papa. They said words like “Maayong buntag” for “Good morning” and “Salamat” for “Thank you.”

One afternoon, Lila and her family walked around their town. Lila noticed the names of places they passed.

“Papa, why is this street called Narra Street?” she asked.

Papa smiled. “It’s named after the narra tree that grows here.”

They walked to the plaza and bought food. Lila pointed at the puto, bibingka, and kakanin.

“Mama, these are Filipino words!” she said.

“Yes,” Mama replied. “These foods come from our culture. Their names come from our local languages.”

Lila felt happy. “Our language is special,” she said.

Mama hugged her. “Yes, Lila. Our language tells the story of who we are.”

Language shows who you are and how you live. At home, you speak your own language and use words that are special to your family. Many streets and places have names from our language, and even our favorite foods have local names. These words help us learn about our community and our culture.

Compare and Contrast

When you compare, you tell how two things are alike or the same.

When you contrast, you tell how the two things are different.

Example:

A cat and a dog are both pets. (comparing)

A cat meows, and a dog barks. (contrasting)

Knowing how to compare and contrast helps you understand stories and concepts better.
