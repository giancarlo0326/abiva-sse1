SPEAKING

Using High-Frequency Words

High-frequency words are words you may see and use a lot when you read and write. Learning these words helps you read faster and write better.

  1. the–a word we use to talk about something specific.
    Example: The cat is sleeping.
  2. you–a word we use to talk to someone.
    Example: You are my friend.
  3. we–a word we use to talk about ourselves and others together.
    Example: We like to play outside.
  4. see–a word we use when we look at something with our eyes.
    Example: I see a bird in the tree.
  5. look–a word we use when we want someone to notice something.
    Example: Look at the rainbow!
  6. in–a word we use to show that something is inside.
    Example: The toy is in the box.
  7. on–a word we use to show that something is on top of something.
    Example: The book is on the table.
  8. can–a word we use to show that someone is able to do something.
    Example: I can jump high.
  9. like–a word we use to show that we enjoy something.
    Example: I like ice cream.
  10. is–a word we use to say about something or someone.
    Example: She is happy.
  11. my–a word we use to show that something belongs to us.
    Example: This is my pencil.

The Dog, the Cat, and the Changing World
Amy R. Pomoy

In a quiet village between the hills and the sea, there lived a dog named Rufo and a cat named Nyang. They were unlikely friends. Rufo loved to run and play in the sun, while Nyang preferred to nap in the shade.

One summer day, the sun felt hotter than ever before. The river where Rufo used to drink began to dry up, and the shady trees where Nyang liked to rest lost their leaves.

Who are the unlikely friends? What happened one summer day? How will Rufo and Nyang feel about this?

“This heat is unbearable!” Nyang complained, flicking her tail. “Even my favorite napping spots are too warm!”

Rufo panted heavily. “The ground burns my paws when I go for walks. The humans keep saying the weather is changing, but why?”

Just then, Wise Old Owl swooped down from his tree. “It’s because of climate change,” he hooted. “The world is getting warmer, and it’s making life harder for everyone—animals and humans alike.”

“What can we do?” asked Rufo.

What answer did the owl give them? What was Rufo thinking of? What do you think they will do next?

Rufo and Nyang decided to help. Rufo led his humans to plant trees in their yard, while Nyang circled her human’s legs until she remembered to turn the faucet and lights off. Rufo and Nyang also got the help of other cats, dogs, and birds in their village. The animals did their part. Slowly, the village grew greener, the river started to flow again, and the trees provided cool shade once more.

One evening, as Rufo lay under a freshly grown tree and Nyang curled up beside him, they smiled.
